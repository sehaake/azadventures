Water Activities

Beaches, rivers, lakes, and swimming holes that give you a break from the desert heat and add water into your Arizona adventures.

Butcher Jones Recreation Site (Beach)

Fort McDowell, AZ ~30 min away

A desert beach area on Saguaro Lake with sand, shallow water access, and mountain views. It is a relaxed spot for picnics, wading, and hanging out by the lake.

Address: 3 E Butcher Jones Beach Rd, Fort McDowell, AZ 85264
Butcher Jones Recreation Site

Salt River Tubing

Mesa, AZ ~45 min away

A classic summer activity where you float down the Salt River on inner tubes. Expect cold water, sun, views of the river canyon, and a very social, all-day experience.

Address: 9200 N Bush Hwy, Mesa, AZ 85215
Salt River Tubing

Paqua Park at Scorpion Bay Marina

Peoria, AZ ~1 hr away

A floating wipeout-style obstacle course on the water at Lake Pleasant. It is bouncy, chaotic, and very fun, especially for groups who want something more playful than just swimming.

Address: 10970 W Peninsula Blvd, Peoria, AZ 85383
Paqua Park

Lake Pleasant

Peoria, AZ ~1 hr away

A large lake northwest of Phoenix with boat rentals, jet skis, kayaks, and scenic coves. You can spend the day on the water, swim from the shore, or book something more unique like a yacht-style boat.

Location: Near Peoria, AZ

Bull Pen Swimming Hole

Camp Verde, AZ ~2 hrs away

A clear-water swimming hole near Camp Verde that you reach after a short hike. From the trailhead it is around a twenty minute walk to the water, where you will find a natural pool and rocky ledges to relax on.

Address: Bull Pen Day Use Area, Bull Pen Rd, Camp Verde, AZ 86322

Fossil Creek

Strawberry, AZ ~2 hrs 15 min away

A blue creek with waterfalls, small caves, and shaded spots along the water. Depending on the route and season, you can drive in on Forest Road 708 from Highway 260 and access one of the eight parking areas, each with short hikes leading down to water area. This option works best with a high-clearance vehicle, especially after monsoon season. For a longer adventure, you can take the Bob Bear Trail, which is a strenuous 10-mile round-trip hike that leads to the waterfall and classic turquoise swimming areas. Either route gives you a mix of clear water, rocky cliffs, and one of the most unique creek systems in Arizona!

Location: Fossil Creek Road (aka Forest Road 708) | Bob Bear Trailhead, near Pine and Strawberry, AZ
